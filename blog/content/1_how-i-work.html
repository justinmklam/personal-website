Adapted from Lifehacker's How I Work interview series. (Last edit: August 17, 2015)

Location: Vancouver, BC

Current gig: Mechanical Design Intern at MistyWest

Current mobile device: Rooted Nexus 4 running Chroma 5.1.1 (Lollipop)

Current computer: Microsoft Surface Pro 3 running Windows 10

One word that best describes how you work: Enthusiastically

What's your workspace like?

The Surface Pro 3 is pretty darn close to having the best of both worlds with the portability of a laptop/tablet and functionality of a desktop. As a student, being able to take notes electronically is a game changer! It's amazingly thin and light to carry around, and I can also plug it into desktop peripherals when things need to get serious. FYI the Pro 3 is surprisingly capable of running SolidWorks!

What apps/software/tools can't you live without?

Apps
  • Google Calendar.  Keeps my entire life organized. 'Nuff said.
  • Google Keep.  I need something to maintain my slurry of lists and unsorted thoughts, and this fits the bill nicely.
  • Pocket + Kobo.  This is a deadly combo.  I can look for articles on my computer or phone (usually with Feedly), save them to Pocket and have them wirelessly sync with my e-reader.  Being able to read long web articles on an e-ink display is a definitive game changer (and blessing for my eyes!).
Software
  • Autohotkey.  Setting or rebinding hotkeys, being able to highlight text from any program and search it on Google, and adding tab scrolling in Chrome are only a few things I use Autohotkey for.  It's sort of amazing.
  • WinSplit Revolution.  Albeit discontinued, it provides an excellent way to manage desktop estate by tiling the windows in any desired configuration.
Tools
  • Logitech G600. I never would have thought a multi-button gaming mouse could change my life.  I can do all computer related tasks with blazing speed now that majority of my mouse move-and-click actions are now binded to one of the 24 available thumb buttons.

What's your favorite to-do list manager?

I'm a long time lover of Google Tasks since it integrates nicely with Google Calendar and Gmail.  In the past, I have probably spent an unproductive amount of time testing different GTD apps when I should have been actually getting things done...

Besides your phone and computer, what gadget can't you live without?

A watch. Sure, a smartphone can tell the time (plus so much more), but I really appreciate the no-frills convenience of simply rotating my wrist to see the time. My trusty Casio 5600E has proven to be an extremely comfortable and bombproof addition to my everyday carry.

What's your best time-saving trick?

Writing things down so I don't have to spend time trying to remember it later on.  I'm partial to having a heavy reliance on my fleeting memory.

What everyday thing are you better at than anyone else?

Staying organized and using hotkeys.  For the latter, I like how they (eventually) become muscle memory and keep my workflow going forward.  My thoughts otherwise become disjointed if I'm constantly switching gears to move my mouse around the screen.

What are you currently reading?

The Martian by Andy Weir. I can't stress enough how thoroughly I've been enjoying it.

What do you listen to while you work?

Deep house or video game soundtracks on Google Music.

Are you more of an introvert or an extrovert?

A bit of both. As much as a recharge from spending time with others, I've been finding myself spending more time doing solo activities. Sometimes it's nice to be able to go at one's own pace!

What's your sleep routine like?

Sometimes I like to pretend I'm an early riser, but weekends reveal my true colours.  I try to get 7-8 hours every night, but I've been known to run off 5 hours in a pinch if I don't think about it too much.

What's the best advice you've ever received?

Don't compare yourself to others.  However, it has become progressively more difficult in this day and age where everyone is showing off their highlight reels on a superfluous number of social media platforms.
